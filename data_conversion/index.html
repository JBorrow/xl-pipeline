Run Description

RUN_DESCRIPTION
Stellar Mass Function (→top)

Stellar mass function binned in equal log-width bins. For a point to be included in the plot, it needs to contain at least three galaxies. Second and third figures show only active and passive galaxies, respectively, but with the same data over-plotted. Galaxies are said to be passive if the have a specific star formation rate of 0.01 / Gyr or lower.

Galaxy Sizes (→top)

Projected galaxy size (100 kpc) aperture. Binned in equal log-width bins. For a point to be included in the plot, it needs to contain at least three galaxies. Left shows all galaxies, center shows only active galaxies, and right shows only passive galaxies. Galaxies are said to be passive if the have a specific star formation rate of 0.01 / Gyr or lower.

Star Formation Rates (→top)

Star formation rate related galaxy properties. For cosmological star formation rate density see here. Specific star formation rate plot only includes active galaxies, whereas the star formation rate plot on the right includes all galaxies. Galaxies are said to be passive if the have a specific star formation rate of 0.01 / Gyr or lower.

Black Holes (→top)

Black-hole related quantities. Note that the stellar velocity dispersion is measured in observations in a fixed 1 kpc aperture. The third figure relates the black hole masses directly to the halo masses, allowing for cross-model comparisons as the halo masses are largely unaffected by baryonic processes.

Stellar Mass-Halo Mass Relation (→top)

Stellar mass-Halo mass relation shown for all galaxies and for centrals only. The third figure shows the ratio between stellar mass and halo mass against halo mass, with the fourth showing the same but plotted against galaxy stellar mass instead.

Star Formation Rate-Halo Mass Relation (→top)

Star formation rate related halo properties. The plots only include active galaxies. Galaxies are said to be passive if the have a specific star formation rate of 0.01 / Gyr or lower.

Tully-Fisher Relation (→top)

Tully-Fisher relation shown for all galaxies.

Gas and Stellar Metallicity (→top)

(Star forming) gas and stellar metallicity of galaxies. All haloes are plotted including subhaloes. Different units are used for each to ensure compatibility with the observational data. A minimum metallicity of 12 + log O/H = 7.5 is imposed for the star-forming gas. Metallicity is measured in the same aperture as the stellar mass. Gallazzi data is corrected from their choice of solar metallicity (0.02) to ours (0.0126).

Histograms (→top)

Histograms showing the number of objects within each bin considered in the above figures. The left three panels show stellar mass, cut by all, then active only, then passive only. The two panels on the right show the halo mass and then the maximal subgrid black hole masses. Note that in the scaling relation figures points are only included if there are three within the appropriate bin.

Density-Temperature (→top)

Density-Temperature diagrams, first shown as a simple histogram (left), and then with the colour showing the mean log metal mass fraction in each bin. Medians are not computed due to their computational cost. The final image shows the internal energy on the vertical axis, instead of the temperature.

Density-Pressure

Density-Pressure diagrams. Medians are not computed due to their computational cost.

Star Formation History and SNIa (→top)

Star formation history plotted directly from the SFR.txt file provided by SWIFT. SNIa rate shown as extracted from SNIa.txt, if available.

Feedback Energy Fraction (→top)

Stellar birth density - metallicity plane. Background shows the feedback energy fraction that these stars will have been born with. This plot shows all stars born over the course of the run. The next figure shows the distribution of $f_E$ with respect to stellar birth density; scatter here is determined by the metallicity dependence. Next to the feedback energy fraction plot, the metal mass fraction and birth density distributions are shown. The dashed line shows the median.

Code Performance (→top)

Several performance metric plots. Note that finding out more detailed information for performance tracking is more difficult in SWIFT and requires running simulations with at least -v 1, but ideally with full task tracking enabled. The first three plots show the evolution of the cost of the simulation as a function of time, with the last one showing how the cost per step depends on the number of updates required in that step.
