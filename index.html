Stellar Mass Function

Stellar mass function binned in equal log-width bins. For a point to be included in the plot, it needs to contain at least three galaxies. Second and third figures show only active and passive galaxies, respectively, but with the same data over-plotted.

Galaxy Sizes

Projected galaxy size (100 kpc) aperture. Binned in equal log-width bins. For a point to be included in the plot, it needs to contain at least three galaxies. Left shows all galaxies, center shows only active galaxies, and right shows only passive galaxies. Galaxies are said to be passive if the have a specific star formation rate of 0.01 / Gyr or lower.

Star Formation Rates

Star formation rate related galaxy properties. For cosmological star formation rate density see here. Specific star formation rate plot only includes active galaxies, whereas the star formation rate plot on the right includes all galaxies. Galaxies are said to be passive if the have a specific star formation rate of 0.01 / Gyr or lower.

Black Holes

Black-hole related quantities. Note that the stellar velocity dispersion is measured in observations in a fixed 1 kpc aperture.

Stellar Mass-Halo Mass Relation

Stellar mass-Halo mass relation shown for all galaxies (left) and for only central galaxies (right).

Tully-Fisher Relation

Tully-Fisher relation shown for all galaxies.

Density-Temperature

Density-Temperature diagrams, first shown as a simple histogram (left), and then with the colour showing the mean log metal mass fraction in each bin. Medians are not computed due to their computational cost.

Star Formation History and SNIa

Star formation history plotted directly from the SFR.txt file provided by SWIFT. SNIa rate shown as extracted from SNIa.txt, if available.

Feedback Energy Fraction

Stellar birth density - metallicity plane. Background shows the feedback energy fraction that these stars will have been born with. This plot shows all stars born over the course of the run. Next to the feedback energy fraction plot, the metal mass fraction and birth density distributions are shown.

Code Performance

Several performance metric plots. Note that finding out more detailed information for performance tracking is more difficult in SWIFT and requires running simulations with at least -v 1, but ideally with full task tracking enabled. The first three plots show the evolution of the cost of the simulation as a function of time, with the last one showing how the cost per step depends on the number of updates required in that step.
